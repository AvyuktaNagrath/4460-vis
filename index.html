Real Economy (GDP)

This treemap shows U.S. economic output by industry, measured as each sector’s share of GDP (production and services).

It illustrates how broadly distributed the real economy is: retail trade, wholesale trade, manufacturing, construction, and professional and business services all contribute significant portions to total output.

These sectors are closely tied to everyday life—jobs, consumption, physical goods, and local services.

The visualization emphasizes that the real economy is structurally diverse and heavily shaped by labor-intensive, ground-level industries rather than a small number of dominant sectors.

Market Value (Cap)

This treemap shows the distribution of U.S. stock market value by sector, measured as each sector’s share of total market capitalization.

Unlike the broad structure of the real economy, the stock market is highly concentrated: information technology, consumer discretionary giants, financials, and healthcare occupy disproportionately large portions of market value.

These sectors benefit from scalability, higher margins, and strong investor growth expectations.

The visualization highlights how financial markets amplify certain industries while underrepresenting others, creating a sector mix that looks very different from the structure of the real economy.
